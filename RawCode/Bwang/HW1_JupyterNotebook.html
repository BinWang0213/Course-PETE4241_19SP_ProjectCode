(a) Vertical stress in MPa¶

The vertical stress can be calculated in onshore and offshore as follows:
$${{S}_{v}}={{\bar{\rho }}_{bulk}}g{{z}_{0}}+\int\limits_{{{z}_{0}}}^{z}{{{\rho }_{bulk}}(z)gdz} \quad \text{Onshore} $$ $$ {{S}_{v}}={{\rho }_{w}}g{{z}_{w}}+{{\bar{\rho }}_{bulk}}g({{z}_{0}}-{{z}_{w}})+\int\limits_{{{z}_{0}}-{{z}_{w}}}^{z}{{{\rho }_{bulk}}(z)gdz} \quad \text{Offshore} $$

(c) Gradient of vertical stress in MPa/km.¶

The gradient is computed using second order accurate central differences in the interior points and either first or second order accurate one-sides (forward or backwards) differences at the boundaries. Here the np.gradient function in numpy library is used. $$ \hat f_{i}^{(1)} = \frac{ \Delta z_{-}^{2}f\left(x_{i} + \Delta z_{+}\right) + \left(\Delta z_{+}^{2} - \Delta z_{-}^{2}\right)f\left(x_{i}\right) - \Delta z_{+}^{2}f\left(x_{i}-\Delta z_{-}\right)} { \Delta z_{-}\Delta z_{+}\left(\Delta z_{+} + \Delta z_{-}\right)} + \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{\Delta z_{+}\Delta z_{-}^{2} + \Delta z_{-}\Delta z_{+}^{2}}{\Delta z_{+} + \Delta z_{-}}\right)$$
